Let's Talk about Sadza!!!






Prep Time:

5 minutes.

Cooking Time:

45 minutes.

Servings:

5 Plates



INGREDIENTS:


  • Maize Meal/ cornmeal - the primary ingredient and the base of Sadza.
  • Water: Used to cook the maize meal and achieve the desired consistency.


INSTRUCTIONS:


  1. Boil water in a large pot or saucepan.
  2. Gradually add maize meal to the boiling water while continuously stirring to prevent lumps from forming.
  3. Lower the heat and continue stirring the mixture for about 15-20 minutes until it thickens and reaches a smooth, porridge-like consistency.
  4. Use a wooden spoon or a traditional wooden paddle called a "tsimeki" to vigorously stir the mixture in a circular motion, ensuring even cooking and preventing it from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  5. Add more maize meal or water if needed to adjust the consistency to your preference. Some prefer a thicker Sadza, while others prefer it slightly thinner.
  6. Continue cooking and stirring for an additional 10-15 minutes until the maize meal is fully cooked and the Sadza is smooth and lump-free.
  7. Once cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes. Serve Sadza hot as a side dish or as a base for various toppings, such as stews, vegetables, or meat dishes.



Note: The cooking time and water-to-maize meal ratio may vary depending on the desired consistency and personal preference. It is advisable to follow a specific recipe for precise measurements and cooking instructions.
